      Bata India Limited is a prominent and iconic footwear company that has been an
    </element>

      integral part of the Indian market for several decades. Established in 1931, Bata has evolved into one of the leading footwear retailers in the country, offering a diverse range of shoes for men, women, and children. With a rich history, a commitment to quality, and a strong presence across India, Bata has become a household name synonymous with trust and durability.The journey of Bata in India began with the opening of its first store in the city of Kolkata. Since then, the brand has expanded its footprint to cover the entire nation, with a vast network of retail outlets, franchise stores, and online platforms. Bata's success can be attributed to its dedication to delivering high-quality footwear that combines comfort, style, and durability. One of the key factors that set Bata apart is its focus on innovation. The company consistently introduces new designs and technologies to meet the evolving needs of its diverse customer base. Bata's product range spans from formal shoes to casual wear, sports shoes to sandals, catering to various occasions and preferences. The brand has successfully adapted to changing fashion trends while ensuring that its products maintain the highest standards of craftsmanship.
    </element>
    <element type="NarrativeText" element_id="cc8cc0409f4c3dd92b8c1b11091f54eb">
      Bata has also been a pioneer in retail strategies, employing a mix of company-owned stores and franchise models to reach customers in urban and rural areas alike. This approach has allowed Bata to establish a widespread presence, making its products accessible to people from all walks of life. Additionally, the brand's online presence has grown significantly, providing customers with the convenience of shopping from the comfort of their homes.
    </element>
    <element type="NarrativeText" element_id="78b658367cf6db00871ef0cb2e2ef139">
      The company's commitment to social responsibility is another aspect that sets it apart. Bata has undertaken various initiatives aimed at contributing to the welfare of society. This includes environmental sustainability efforts, ethical business practices, and community development projects. Bata's corporate social responsibility programs reflect its dedication to being a responsible and conscientious corporate citizen. Over the years, Bata has witnessed several milestones and achievements. The brand has consistently adapted to market dynamics, incorporating feedback from customers to enhance its product offerings. Bata has also collaborated with renowned designers and celebrities to create exclusive collections, further establishing its presence in the fashion industry.In terms of manufacturing, Bata has state-of-the-art facilities that adhere to international quality standards. The company's emphasis on quality control ensures that every pair of shoes that bears the Bata logo meets stringent criteria, providing customers with a sense of trust and reliability.Despite facing challenges in a

    <element type="Title" element_id="0f542b515f3e2c5aa98ea9f4fb37f6c0">
      Introduction:
    </element>
    <element type="NarrativeText" element_id="b054604ff57675fabc94d13dd30208ac">
      Capital Asset Pricing Model (CAPM), its assumptions, formula, and applications .
    </element>
    <element type="Title" element_id="bb751dd3e9826ea83aa9e09b623e1693">
      CAPM (Capital Asset Pricing Model):
    </element>
    <element type="Title" element_id="c6d99908dcb625495bc2dd29755a9f1e">
      Assumptions:
    </element>
    <element type="NarrativeText" element_id="03273e4af7b30f084bc230a067168463">
      1. Perfect Capital Markets: Assumes no taxes, transaction costs, or restrictions on the flow of information, allowing for frictionless trading.
    </element>
    <element type="NarrativeText" element_id="245470318586d1cdc00c31f83a168a86">
      2. Homogeneous Expectations: Assumes all investors have the same expectations regarding future returns, standard deviations, and correlations.
    </element>
    <element type="NarrativeText" element_id="c99beba105220d232cf3e4ec00456887">
      3. Risk-Free Rate: Assumes the existence of a risk-free rate at which investors can lend or borrow money without any risk.
    </element>
    <element type="NarrativeText" element_id="55b3a9768106a51f754723de76edd173">
      4. Single Holding Period: Assumes investors hold securities for a single period without intermediate consumption.
    </element>
    <element type="NarrativeText" element_id="733005b1b6989c51dc67ceea636876b4">
      5. Investors are Price Takers: Assumes investors' buying or selling activities do not influence security prices.
    </element>
    <element type="Title" element_id="20260edb6f5afbcd8910b665c3a57a55">
      Formula:
    </element>
    <element type="Title" element_id="c503a4c1575ca6d23c0264bc23ba52ca">
      The CAPM formula is given by:
    </element>
    <element type="Title" element_id="c49b91214fec42f2ebe620173d9c33b6">
      [ Ri = Rf + \beta_i (R_m - R_f) \]
    </element>
    <element type="Title" element_id="112b749fa1924888439c31620de54ae4">
      Where:
    </element>
    <element type="NarrativeText" element_id="f54d805faec320c8d086355d82c387bd">
      - \( R_i \) is the expected return on the investment. - \( R_f \) is the risk-free rate. - \( \beta_i \) is the beta of the investment, representing its systematic risk. - \( R_m \) is the expected return of the market. - \( (R_m - R_f) \) is the market risk premium.
    </element>
    <element type="Title" element_id="95bd0af7a59da078c2d558c09b6c63c8">
      Applications:
    </element>
    <element type="NarrativeText" element_id="f0de0982da98205eb4b66248b68b295c">
      1. Expected Return Estimation: Used to estimate the expected return on an investment based on its systematic risk relative to the market.
    </element>
    <element type="NarrativeText" element_id="3120fc6b2615a6ade888deb063b96261">
      2. Cost of Capital: Determines the cost of equity for companies, aiding in project evaluation and investment decision- making.
    </element>
    <element type="NarrativeText" element_id="df5615638d248eb899fdcae22bf6394e">
      3. Valuation: Employed in the valuation of companies by discounting future cash flows using the cost of equity derived from CAPM.
    </element>
    <element type="NarrativeText" element_id="ba64f000a816369916791ac7dfeb8a76">
      4. Portfolio Management: Helps assess the expected return and risk of a portfolio based on individual asset characteristics.
    </element>
    <element type="NarrativeText" element_id="8baa55e5b6e439c87257094477f018c4">
      5. Risk Management: Assists in understanding and managing systematic risk associated with an investment, aiding in risk-return trade-off decisions.
    </element>
