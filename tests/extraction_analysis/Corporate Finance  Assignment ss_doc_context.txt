[Title]
Introduction:

[NarrativeText]
Capital Asset Pricing Model (CAPM), its assumptions, formula, and applications .

[Title]
CAPM (Capital Asset Pricing Model):

[Title]
Assumptions:

[NarrativeText]
1. Perfect Capital Markets: Assumes no taxes, transaction costs, or restrictions on the flow of information, allowing for frictionless trading.

[NarrativeText]
2. Homogeneous Expectations: Assumes all investors have the same expectations regarding future returns, standard deviations, and correlations.

[NarrativeText]
3. Risk-Free Rate: Assumes the existence of a risk-free rate at which investors can lend or borrow money without any risk.

[NarrativeText]
4. Single Holding Period: Assumes investors hold securities for a single period without intermediate consumption.

[NarrativeText]
5. Investors are Price Takers: Assumes investors' buying or selling activities do not influence security prices.

[Title]
Formula:

[Title]
The CAPM formula is given by:

[Title]
[ Ri = Rf + \beta_i (R_m - R_f) \]

[Title]
Where:

[NarrativeText]
- \( R_i \) is the expected return on the investment. - \( R_f \) is the risk-free rate. - \( \beta_i \) is the beta of the investment, representing its systematic risk. - \( R_m \) is the expected return of the market. - \( (R_m - R_f) \) is the market risk premium.

[Title]
Applications:

[NarrativeText]
1. Expected Return Estimation: Used to estimate the expected return on an investment based on its systematic risk relative to the market.

[NarrativeText]
2. Cost of Capital: Determines the cost of equity for companies, aiding in project evaluation and investment decision- making.

[NarrativeText]
3. Valuation: Employed in the valuation of companies by discounting future cash flows using the cost of equity derived from CAPM.

[NarrativeText]
4. Portfolio Management: Helps assess the expected return and risk of a portfolio based on individual asset characteristics.

[NarrativeText]
5. Risk Management: Assists in understanding and managing systematic risk associated with an investment, aiding in risk-return trade-off decisions.
